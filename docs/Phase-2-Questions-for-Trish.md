## 1. The Big Metaphor: One Gem, a Collection, or a Bracelet?

You raised this multiple times and it's the single most important creative decision in Phase 2. There are three distinct paths:

### Option A — One Evolving Diamond
Each trainee has one personal gem. It starts rough and beautiful. Facets appear as courses are completed. Shadowing moments add internal clarity and luminosity. At program completion: a fully cut, brilliant diamond — uniquely shaped by their journey. No two are identical.

**Strength**: The strongest "narrative of becoming." One stone, one identity, one story.
**Risk**: Can feel abstract. Hard to see progress at a glance when you're mid-journey.

### Option B — Gem Collection in a Jewel Box
Each course (Services, Diamonds, Client Experience, etc.) earns a distinct gem when completed. Different courses produce different coloured gems. They collect into a jewel box that fills over time.

**Strength**: Tangible, collectible. Easy to see what's earned and what's still to come. Maps to completionism.
**Risk**: Loses the "single identity" narrative. Feels more like collecting achievements than becoming someone.

### Option C — Charm Bracelet
Each course completion adds a charm to a bracelet. Each charm is a miniature object connected to the course — a loupe for Diamonds, a ring for Services, a welcome card for Client Experience.

**Strength**: Deeply on-brand for a jewellery house. Personal, wearable, beautiful. The team would intuitively understand it. Could even become a physical reward (a real Canturi charm bracelet that mirrors the digital one).

**Risk**: The charm metaphor is warmer but less "transformative" than the diamond metaphor. It's about collecting, not becoming.

### A Possible Hybrid
The single diamond lives on the home screen as the trainee's overall identity — it gains luminosity from shadowing and facets from mastery levels. But *separately*, each course completion earns a collectible gem/charm displayed in a collection view. Two systems: one for identity, one for collection. Best of both worlds, but more to build.

**The question**: Which sentence would Farhana say to Vicky with more pride?
- *"Look — my diamond has five facets now"*
- *"I've earned five gems in my jewel box"*
- *"My bracelet has five charms already"*

Which one feels most Canturi?

## 2. Mastery Language: Craft Vocabulary or Gemstones?

Two directions were raised in the brief:

### Craft Vocabulary
Apprentice → Artisan → Specialist → Expert → **Canturi Standard**

### Gemstone Progression
Topaz → Aquamarine → Sapphire → Ruby → Diamond → Yellow Diamond → Pink Diamond

### Our Recommendation
**Craft vocabulary**, for three reasons:

1. It directly mirrors the real career path you described (Host → Consultant → Specialist → Senior Specialist). The language reinforces actual career progression, not just training milestones.

2. **"Canturi Standard"** as the pinnacle is extraordinarily powerful. It means something beyond the app — it says "you are now Canturi in this area." A gemstone name can't carry that weight.

3. If the central visual metaphor is already a diamond/gem, and the mastery levels are *also* gemstones, the language gets muddy. "I'm a Ruby in Diamonds" is confusing. "I'm Expert in Diamonds" is clear and meaningful.

The gemstone names could still live in the visual layer — the gem's colour or type could change with mastery level — but the *language* the trainee sees and says should be the craft vocabulary.

**Question**: Does "Canturi Standard" feel right as the ultimate level for *every* course? Or should some courses cap at Expert, with Canturi Standard reserved for the most significant domains?

## 3. What Triggers a Milestone Moment?

The brief noted "TBC" on whether a facet/gem appears per Course or per Category.

Given the hierarchy: **Workshop → Course → Category (individual training item)**

| Trigger Level | Frequency | Emotional Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Per Category | Dozens of moments | Frequent but risks feeling small |
| Per Course | 7–10 moments | Significant, each one feels earned |
| Per Workshop | 2–3 moments | Monumental but too infrequent |

### Our Suggestion
The gem/charm is earned per **Course** completion (reaching Canturi Standard in that course). Individual category completions contribute *visible progress toward* the course gem — a shimmer, a glow, a facet outline appearing — but the full gem only materialises when the course is complete.

This gives both:
- **Frequent micro-feedback** (each category adds a visible spark)
- **Significant milestone moments** (the full gem appearing is rare and earned)

**Question**: How many courses do you envision in the full program? This determines whether per-course gems feel achievable and special (7–10 is ideal) or overwhelming (20+).

## 4. Career Milestones: Host → Consultant → Specialist → Senior Specialist

You described a real career progression that's separate from training completion but deeply connected to it. This is one of the most interesting parts of the brief.

### The Key Design Question
Should the Training Studio:

**(a)** Surface readiness to the manager? — *"Farhana has completed all Consultant-level training requirements"* — visible to Vicky but not to Farhana. The manager delivers the news in person.

**(b)** Show the trainee their path? — Farhana can see the milestones ahead: *"Consultant readiness: 7 of 9 courses complete."* She knows what she's working toward.

**(c)** Both — Manager sees readiness. Trainee sees progress toward next role. But the actual role change is always a human moment.

### Our Recommendation
Option (c) — with care. The trainee sees the path (*"Your journey toward Consultant"*) because aspiration is motivating. The manager sees the readiness signal. But the system never announces *"You are now a Consultant"* — that's Vicky's moment to deliver, face to face, in the boutique. The system whispers to the manager; the manager delivers the news.

**Question**: Should certain courses or content be locked until a role level is reached? e.g. Advanced Consultation content only available to Consultants? Or should everything be visible but sequenced by the manager?

## 5. Canturi Reward Dollars — How Deep?

The existing reward system ($100 for Google reviews, $6500 for annual sales milestones) is a real, tangible motivator. Two approaches:

### Light Integration
The Training Studio surfaces "reward-worthy moments" — first Canturi Standard, all courses at Artisan+, workshop completion. But the actual reward issuance stays with you and the managers. The system says *"Milestone reached"* — you decide if it warrants dollars.

**Advantage**: Keeps the surprise. Rewards feel personal, not automatic. You retain full control.

### Deep Integration
The system tracks reward balances, auto-issues for certain milestones, shows a running total redeemable for jewellery.

**Advantage**: Fully automated. But risks making rewards feel transactional, which directly conflicts with the brief's "no points currency" principle.

### Our Recommendation
**Light integration for Phase 2.** Training milestones naturally create reward-worthy moments, and you decide which ones to attach dollars to. If rewards become automatic, they become expected — and expected rewards lose their power. The moment Trish or Vicky *chooses* to reward is far more meaningful than an automated notification.

**Question**: Are there specific training milestones you already know should trigger rewards? Or should this stay flexible and at your discretion?

## 6. Shadowing Quality: How to Encourage Depth Without Homework

You emphasised this strongly and rightly: shadowing notes must be rich — insights, questions, genuine reflection. Not just "done."

### The Design Challenge
How do you encourage quality without making it feel like a compliance exercise?

### Ideas We'd Like Your Reaction To

1. **The invisible quality mechanic**: Shadowing entries with richer, more detailed notes contribute more luminosity to the trainee's gem. The trainee notices their gem glows brighter after a thoughtful entry — but the system never *tells them* this rule. They discover it through experience. This rewards quality without measuring it.

2. **The three prompt questions are already excellent.** Could a fourth *optional* question add depth? Something like: *"If you were showing this to a new team member one day, what would you tell them?"* — this reframes the trainee as a future mentor, which is powerful.

3. **Manager visibility**: Should Vicky see a "depth indicator" on shadowing entries? Or does that create surveillance anxiety that undermines psychological safety?

4. **No minimum word count.** This feels corporate and punishing. A two-sentence entry that captures the right insight is worth more than a long paragraph of filler.

**Question**: How does Vicky currently coach Farhana on the quality of her observations? Is it conversational? Is there a structure? We should mirror what works in person.

## 7. The "Willy Wonka" Moment — Visible Aspiration or Hidden Surprise?

A trip to the Sydney Creation Department as the ultimate reward is a brilliant idea. It bridges digital achievement with a physical, unforgettable experience.

Two approaches:

### Visible North Star
Build it into the system as the ultimate aspiration. The trainee sees it from Day 1: *"Complete your full training journey and earn a visit to where Canturi jewellery is born."* It's always there, always drawing them forward.

**Advantage**: Creates a powerful long-term motivator. The team talks about it. It becomes part of Canturi culture.

### Hidden Surprise
The trainee doesn't know it exists until they've earned it. The purest form of the Hidden Unlockable mechanic. One day they open the app and see something they never expected.

**Advantage**: Genuinely unforgettable. The surprise itself becomes a story that travels across boutiques. "Did you hear what happened when Farhana finished her training?"

### Our Instinct
The hidden surprise is more aligned with the brief's philosophy — but only if Trish is comfortable with it remaining a surprise. If the team *needs* to know the aspiration exists to be motivated by it, make it visible. If the team is already motivated by the work itself (which the brief suggests), the surprise is more powerful.

**Question**: Which approach feels right for your team? And is this something you'd want to commit to for every trainee who completes the full program, or keep as a discretionary reward?

## 8. Plate Rules: Daily Minimums, Auto-Transfer, and Diversity

You specified: minimum 3, maximum 6 items per day. Diversity across courses. Untouched items auto-transfer.

### Clarifications Needed

**a) Does the minimum of 3 apply every day?**
Some days may be pure client days — doors open, it's busy until close, no training opportunity. Should the manager be able to set a "client day" with no plate? Or does every working day require at least 3?

**b) Auto-transfer behaviour:**
If an item has been untouched for, say, 5 consecutive days, should the system flag it to the manager? *"This item may need reassignment or a conversation."* This prevents items silently rolling forward forever.

**c) The diversity rule:**
Should the system enforce it (prevent assigning 4 items from the same course) or suggest it? e.g. *"Today's plate is heavy on Services — consider adding variety?"* — a whisper, not a block.

**d) Weekends and days off:**
Do plates exist for non-working days? Or does the system know the boutique's operating days?
